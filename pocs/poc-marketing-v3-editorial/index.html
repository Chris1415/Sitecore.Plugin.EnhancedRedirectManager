Editor's note

— page i

Most software speaks loudly about itself. It announces uptime and revenue and engagement and the small percent improvements that the people who write the dashboards have come to require of it. Redirect Manager does not. It is, by nature, a quiet thing — a gentle hand on the URL, a steady traffic cop at the seam of a site migration. Its goal is to disappear.

What follows is an account of the three surfaces through which the editor may meet it: the Full Page, where the work is laid out like a long-form essay; the Panel, which whispers from the side of a page detail; and the Widget, a dashboard glance in the editor's morning routine. Each was composed with the conviction that the best redirect is the one nobody notices.
