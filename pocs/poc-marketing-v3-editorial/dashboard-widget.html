On the widget

The widget is the smallest of our three forms. It sits among a dozen other tiles on the morning dashboard, and is meant to be read in the time it takes to take a sip.

A good widget reports a single thing well. Ours reports the count: how many redirects, presently, are doing their quiet work across this tenant. Below that, the small sparkline, like a heart monitor at the bedside of a patient who is sleeping peacefully.

On numbers, written

The Chicago Manual recommends prose for numbers under one hundred. We extend the courtesy further — through the thousands, where a number begins to feel real.

It is a small thing, but a generous one, to render a count both ways. Numerals are efficient, but they wear thin. Prose slows the reader, just briefly, and asks them to feel the shape of the figure. Twelve hundred and forty-seven. Read aloud, it has weight. 1,247 is a fact. Prose is a meaning.

The dashboard widget, for that reason, prints both — and beneath them, the sparkline, which is a sentence without words.
